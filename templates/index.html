Welcome to the Johari Window Generator

An app to help improve self-awareness within your community of friends, family, colleagues, teams, etc.


Don't have an account? Sign up here.

Were you given a key by an existing user? Submit data here.

Instructions

Step 1. Create a username/password and then choose a key to share with others so they can find you. Then you'll select adjectives from a list that YOU feel describe YOURSELF.

Step 2. Share your username and shareable key with others so they can select adjectives from the same list that THEY feel describe YOU.

Step 3. Login to your profile to see your Johari Window. The window will show the adjectives that:
  • You both said describe you.
  • Only they (and not you) chose describe you.
  • Only you (and no one else) chose to describe you.
  • Neither you nor others chose to describe you.
  The app will show you more about what you're putting out there for others to see.

Sample Window

Scenario:Jim works on a team with 6 people, but feels their reception of him is inconsistent. Though he connected with some of his teammembers right away, with others he has more trouble finding his "in" and feels he might be too shy to put himself out there or that these particular teammembers just plain don't like him. Jim uses the Johari Window to build more self-awareness with in his team and find out what qualities he is really putting out there.

Analysis:Jim's Arena indicates qualities both he and his teammates see in him. Jim and his team have strongly identified him as Able, Accepting, Calm, Helpful, Modest, and Proud, and weakly identified him as Kind, Loving, Reflective and Warm. Jim's Blind Spot indicates qualities his team sees that Jim does not see in himself. His team has strongly identified him as Confident, Observant, Extroverted, Bold and Brave. Jim's Facade indicates qualities he sees in himself but that his team does not see in him. The team does not see him as Intervorted, Nervous, Quiet, Searching, Self-conscious, Shy, Silly, or Tense. Jim's Unknown indicates qualities neither he nor his team sees in him. He and his team do not see him as Adaptable, Energetic, Self-Assertive, or Spontaneous.

Conclusion:Jim's Joahri Window tells him that he has an inflated sense of his own introvert nature. They see more of his extroverted nature, but he could make more of an effort to adapt to his team environment by putting more energy into making his kind and warm nature known to more members of his team. Perhaps his modesty (which is high in his arena) is in his way. Perhaps gestures don't need to be as planned as he thought, and he could be a little more spontaneous in how he connects with his team. If Jim lets his guard down, asserts himself more as interested in connecting, he may be able to move past his introverted facade and become a more supportive and more present member of his team.
